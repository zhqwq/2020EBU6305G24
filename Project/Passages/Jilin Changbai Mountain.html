Changbai Mountain, Jilin

The main peak of the Changbai Mountain Range is located in Changbai Korean Autonomous County, Baishan City, Jilin Province, and is the birthplace of the Yalu, Songhua and Tumen rivers. It is the birthplace of the Manchu people in China and the sacred mountain of Manchu culture. The word "Changbai" in the Changbai Mountains also has a beautiful connotation of longevity and love, representing the aspiration and celebration of faithful and fulfilling love. Changbai Mountain was first recorded in Chinese writing over 4,000 years ago, in the Shanhaijing, it was called "Buhan Mountain", in the Northern Wei it was called "Tu Tai Mountain", in the Tang it was called "Taibai Mountain", and in Jin it was called It was called "Changbai Mountain" in the Jin Dynasty.

The highest peak of Changbai Mountain is General Peak in North Korea, at 2,749 metres above sea level. The highest peak in China is Baiyunfeng, at 2,691m, the highest peak in northeast China.

The Changbai Mountains in a narrow sense refers to the main peaks and veins of the Changbai Mountain Range, which includes the main peaks of the Changbai Mountains. In a broader sense, the Changbai Mountains refer to the eastern mountains of Liaoning, Jilin and Heilongjiang provinces in China, as well as the many remaining ranges in the Russian Far East and the Korean Peninsula. Changbai Mountain, with its Tianchi, waterfalls, snow sculptures, forest sea, etc., has been listed in the Guinness Book of World's Best, among which are the Ten Most Famous Mountains in China, the Five Great Lakes in China and the Ten Great Forests in China.
