East Lake, Wuhan

Wuhan East Lake is a tourist attraction with a large natural lake as the core, the lake and mountains as the characteristics, set tourism and sightseeing, leisure and holiday, science education as the main function, receiving millions of tourists at home and abroad every year, is the largest scenic excursion in central China, was the largest city lake in China before 2014, in 2014 due to the expansion of Wuhan central city, East Lake relegated to the Jiangxia District of Wuhan City after the Townsend Lake, is The second largest city lake in China.

Named after its location in the eastern part of Wuchang in Wuhan, Hubei Province, East Lake is now one of the most extensive city lakes in China, with a water area of 33 square kilometres, six times that of West Lake in Hangzhou. Located on the southern bank of the Yangtze River, it was formed by the silting up of the Yangtze River and was connected to other lakes in Wuchang and to the Yangtze River more than 100 years ago and was subject to frequent flooding. With manual intervention, the East Lake and its surrounding lakes have since been separated from the Yangtze River.

The Wuhan East Lake Scenic Area is located between Wuhan's second and third ring roads in the urban area of Wuhan. The scenic area covers 73 square kilometres, of which the lake covers 33 square kilometres, making it the second largest city lake in China. Together with the land scenic area along the lake, it covers an area of more than 80 square kilometres. The winding shores of the lake, with its interlocking harbours and branches, are known as the Ninety-Nine Bays. There are more than 100 scenic spots, 12 large and small lakes, more than 120 islands, 112 km of winding shoreline, 34 peaks around the lake, and more than 10,000 mu of mountain forests.
