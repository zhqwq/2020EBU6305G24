Yellow Crane Tower, Wuhan

The Yellow Crane Tower is a famous historical spot in China and abroad, together with Hunan Yueyanglou, Jiangxi Tengwangge and Shandong Penglai Pavilion, the four most famous buildings in China, known as "the first building of the world's rivers and mountains". It is located on the banks of the Yangtze River, at the head of the Snake Mountain, with the backdrop of Wuchang City, facing the raging Yangtse River, and the relatively elegant and handsome Qingchuan Pavilion, just at the intersection of the Yangtze River and the BeijingGuangzhou line, i.e. the intersection of the east-west waterway and the north-south landway. Famous scholars of the past generations, such as Cui Hao, Li Bai, Bai Juyi, Jia Dao, Lu You, Yang Shen and Zhang Juzheng, have all come here for fun and poetry. They left behind many popular poems. In the Tang Dynasty, the poet Cui Hao wrote, "The people of the past have already taken the Yellow Crane, and there is no Yellow Crane Tower left here." The Yellow Crane Tower has gained a great reputation.

The Yellow Crane Tower is said to have been built during the Three Kingdoms period, and has been destroyed and built time and again over the ages. The site of the building is still at the head of the Snake Hill. The main building is 49 metres high, with five storeys, with a pointed roof and flying eaves. The eaves of each storey are directly related to the name of the building, which is shaped like a yellow crane, spreading its wings to fly. The whole building is majestic, yet refined, and rich in rhythm and beauty of change. The eaves on the ground floor are 30 metres in diameter, and the central hall has a large relief carving on the front wall, showing myths and legends about the Yellow Crane Tower through the ages; the third floor has a mezzanine corridor, displaying poems and paintings; the second, third and fourth floors have a four-sided corridor for visitors to look out over the river; the fifth floor is the observation hall, where you can enjoy the view of the river. Outside the building, there are a number of auxiliary buildings such as the cast bronze cranes, pagodas, pagodas, corridors and pavilions, which make the main building even more magnificent. The stunning scenery of the three towns of Wuhan can be seen from the top of the tower, and the beautiful mountains and rivers of the vast China can be seen from afar. Due to this unique geographical location, as well as the poems, writings, couplets, plaques, rock carvings and folk tales passed down from previous generations, the Yellow Crane Tower has become a famous cultural building where mountains and rivers and humanistic landscapes rely on each other.
