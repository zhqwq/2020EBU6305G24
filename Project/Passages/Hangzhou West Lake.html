East Lake, Hangzhou

Location Area
West Lake is located at No.1 Longjing Road, West Lake District, Hangzhou City, Zhejiang Province, west of Hangzhou City, with a total area of 49 square kilometres and a lake surface area of 6.38 square kilometres, with the central geographical coordinates of 30°14′45″ North latitude and 120°8′30″ East longitude.

Geology and geomorphology
The West Lake is surrounded by mountains on the south, west and north, the east is adjacent to the city, and the south is adjacent to the Qiantang River, the White Embankment, Su Causeway, Yang Gong Causeway and Zhao Gong Causeway divide the lake into several water surfaces, and there are three islands in the lake. "The first level of the West Lake and the hills is the one with the most beautiful scenery. The West Lake is connected to the first level of the mountains, and the distances to the last three levels are 1650m, 3450m and 5600m respectively, and the elevation angle is within 5° when looking at the mountains in the lake. Hydrological features

The catchment area of West Lake is 21.22 square kilometres, with an annual runoff of 14 million cubic metres in the basin and a storage capacity of nearly 14 million cubic metres, with a natural alternation of water of 1 time/year. The West Lake has a nearly oval shape in profile with a relatively flat bottom. The natural surface water sources of the lake are four streams, namely Jinshajian, Longhongjian, Chishanjian (Huiyinjian) and Changqiaoxi. The water level of the lake is maintained at the Yellow Sea elevation of 7.15m, ±0.05m, with a maximum water level of 7.70m and a minimum water level of 6.92m, with a difference of 50cm between high and low. The reservoir capacity is about 14,294,000 cubic metres. The average depth of the lake is 2.27m, the deepest is about 5m and the shallowest is less than 1m. The average annual lake precipitation of the lake is 5.629 million cubic metres. The flushing coefficient of the water system is 1.49, and when the gates are closed during the dry season, the flow rate is equal to 0. Even during the flood period, the general flow rate is only below 0.05 m/s. The West Lake draws water from the Qiantang River, with a volume of about 120 million cubic metres per year.

Climatic characteristics West Lake is located at the edge of the hills of southeast China and the northern edge of the subtropics, with an average annual total solar radiation between 100-110 kcal/cm2, 1800- 2100 hours of sunshine and plenty of light, an average annual temperature of 16.2°C and an average annual frost-free period of 245 days. There are four distinct seasons throughout the year, with sunny and rainy seasons, significant alternating winter and summer winds, abundant rainfall, annual precipitation of about 1500 mm, and humid air. The West Lake ecosystem can be divided into three subsystems: forest ecosystem, freshwater ecosystem and wetland ecosystem, with the forest ecosystem being the main one.
