Beijing Old Summer Palace

Yuanmingyuan, known as "the model of all gardening arts" and "the garden of ten thousand gardens", was once famous for its grand regional scale, outstanding construction skills, exquisite architectural landscape, rich cultural collection and profound national cultural connotation.

Yuanmingyuan is located in Haidian, the western suburb of Beijing, close to the summer palace. It was built in 1707 in the 46th year of the reign of Emperor Kangxi and consists of Yuanming, Changchun and Qichun gardens. It covers an area of 350 hectares (more than 5200 mu), of which the water surface area is about 140 hectares (2100 mu). It has more than 100 garden landscapes, with a construction area of more than 160000 square meters. It is a large royal palace built and operated by the emperors of the Qing Dynasty in more than 150 years.

Yuanmingyuan inherits the excellent gardening tradition of China for more than 3000 years. It not only has the elegance of palace architecture, but also has the euphemism of Jiangnan waterside gardens. At the same time, it absorbs the European garden architectural forms, integrates different styles of garden architecture, and makes people feel harmonious and perfect in the overall layout. It can be said that "although it is done by people, it is just like the sky".

In 1860, the British and French allied forces invaded Beijing and burned down the Old Summer Palace. In 1900, the Allied forces of the eight powers invaded Beijing to burn, kill and plunder. Empress Dowager Cixi fled to Xi'an with Emperor Guangxu. The soldiers of the eight banners, bandits and local ruffians took advantage of the fire to demolish nearly 100 buildings that were left and basically restored in succession, which completely destroyed the buildings and ancient trees of Yuanmingyuan. After that, the relics of Yuanmingyuan were seized by bureaucrats, warlords, profiteers and even damaged by the government. The influential officials of the Beiyang government, including some who are responsible for the protection of the Yuanmingyuan site, rely on their power to transport stone carvings and Taihu Lake stones from the Yuanmingyuan to build their houses.
